💜 Emotional Tone (pick up to 3)

The emotional character of the song — what it asks of the partnership. Pick up to 3 that feel right.

  • Playful — Fun, cheeky, musical games between partners.
  • Sultry — Slow burn, tension, delayed gratification.
  • Melancholic — Longing, bittersweet, introspective.
  • Aggressive — Driving energy, sharp, assertive.
  • Romantic — Warm, connected, intimate.
  • Dark — Brooding, intense, edgy undertones.
  • Light — Airy, uplifting, feel-good groove.
  • Triumphant — Big, swelling builds; great for showcase endings.
  • Nostalgic — Warm retro soul; classic blues/Motown DNA.
  • Cinematic — Dramatic arc, big production, soundtrack-level scope.
  • Hypnotic — Repetitive groove that pulls you in; rewards elasticity.

How fast the song feels to dance — not just the BPM. Two songs at 110 BPM can dance completely differently.

  • Slow — stretchy, breathy, bluesy. Lots of room for connection and delay.
  • Medium — groove-heavy, conversational. The WCS sweet spot.
  • Fast — reactive, playful, athletic. Less time for decoration.

🔄 Phrase Predictability

How reliably the song resolves its musical phrases on the expected count.

  • 1 – Very Predictable — Clean 8-counts, textbook phrasing. Great for beginners learning to hear music.
  • 3 – Moderate — Some surprises, mostly lands where you expect it.
  • 5 – Deceptive — Fake-outs, irregular phrase lengths, delayed resolutions. Rewards dancers who truly listen.

⚡ Break Behavior

How the song handles musical breaks — moments where the groove pauses or shifts. This is huge for WCS musicality.

  • Clear breaks — Obvious, well-telegraphed pauses. Easy to hit.
  • Fake breaks — Sounds like a break is coming, then keeps going. Tests your patience.
  • Micro-pauses — Brief, subtle gaps in the groove. Rewards dancers who breathe with the music.
  • Sustained silence — Long, dramatic pauses. High reward for stillness and connection.
  • No obvious breaks — Constant groove throughout. Dance the texture instead.

🎯 Accent Sharpness

How hard-edged the song's accents and hits are. This shapes what kind of body movement the music invites.

  • Smooth (1) — Legato, flowing. Encourages stretch, delayed anchors, liquid movement.
  • Balanced (3) — Mix of flow and definition. Works for both styles.
  • Punchy (5) — Sharp staccato accents. Rewards body hits, stops, rhythmic footwork, sharp arm styling.

🌊 Elasticity Potential

A WCS-specific concept. How much the song rewards stretch — waiting, expanding into the space the music gives you.

  • Low (1) — Constant, driving pulse. Less room to breathe. Dance on top of the beat.
  • High (4–5) — The song breathes. Rushing feels wrong. Patience and delay create magic. These songs punish leaders who push and reward followers who wait.

High elasticity songs are where WCS connection truly shines.

🎲 Risk Level (Competition)

Will this song elevate you — or expose you? Risk factors include deceptive phrasing, irregular breaks, tempo shifts, and layered rhythms.

  • Safe (1–2) — Predictable, forgiving. Great for prelims or when you want to shine consistently.
  • Moderate (3) — Some gotchas, but manageable for experienced dancers.
  • High Risk (4–5) — Irregular, deceptive, unforgiving. In the right hands: spectacular. In the wrong hands: a disaster. Ask yourself: "Do I own this song?"
